## Methods

To reach this level, one must cultivate at least one Essence to completion. There are two methods to doing this, both of which will have lasting consequences on the Third Step as a whole:

The first method is significantly easier but presents one fatal disadvantage. This method involves using Joss Flames to refine and grow an Essence. While a cultivator can practice multiple Essences in this path, once they have opened the Great Void Gate and formed a body of laws for any one Essence, they will be unable to ever reach the Third Step with any new ones. However, with each Essence that they have before breaking though to the third step, they will have a chance to open a second or more gates, with increasing difficulty, this increases their power and potential when they reach the third step at the end of the process, but they once again will not be able to cultivate any new Essences to completion after they truly reach the Third Step.

The second method involves cultivating Essence without Joss Flames, this is much harder as it means they will have to rely on their own insights. However, there will be no limits on how many Essences they can nurture even after reaching the Third Step, allowing them to reach higher levels through the attainment of a new Essence instead of constantly supplementing the same one forever. Those who practice this method will reach the peak of Nirvana Void with 4 Essences, the peak of Spirit Void with 8 Essences and the peak of Arcane Void with 9 Essences that have been fused into one again, forming a complete cycle.

### Techniques

It is said that a Third Step cultivator can merge with the World and create a Dao Avatar to go anywhere.

### Trivia

The second method of cultivating Essence is essentially the same as to the Dao Realm that replaces the Third Step in Er Gen's later novel, I Shall Seal The Heavens, as it involves cultivating 9 Essences.
