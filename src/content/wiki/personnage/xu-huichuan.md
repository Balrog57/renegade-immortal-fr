## Overview

The warden of Xu, the warden of Hong Prison, oversees a specialized section of the prison where inmates are hand-picked and destined for execution. He cultivates the Demon Slaughter Dao , a path that refines the heart through slaughter and killing intent. Xu emphasizes that immersing oneself fully in slaughter is essential to forging a powerful killing heart, the foundation of his Dao. His cultivation level is comparable to Mo Lihai’s, both reaching the peak late stage of Soul Transformation .

Recognizing Wang Lin’s extraordinary potential and calm disposition, Xu offers to help him cultivate his own slaughter spell. However, he sets a condition: Wang Lin must assist him in killing the demon general Shi Xiao using the slaughter spell, thereby allowing Shi Xiao to die in accordance with the path of slaughter. Since Xu cannot leave Hong Prison and Shi Xiao would never enter it voluntarily, this cooperation is necessary.

Xu personally guides Wang Lin deeper into Hong Prison , especially into the underground sections where the concentration of killing intent and resentment is far stronger. He explains that the prison’s visible structure is largely a facade; the true purpose of Hong Prison’s four divisions in Sky Demon City is to generate killing intent to fuel the Demon Emperor’s sword . Xu warns that merely killing ordinary prisoners will not produce sufficient murderous intent for true advancement, implying that Wang Lin must experience real slaughter to cultivate his Dao fully.

Despite the oppressive and hostile environment, Xu has adapted over hundreds of years and demonstrates both strategic acumen and patience. As their cooperation progresses, he leads Wang Lin into increasingly dangerous, darker regions of the prison, where the killing aura is strongest.

At the conclusion of Wang Lin’s time in Hong Prison, the Emperor’s sword an immensely powerful entity sustained by the prison’s killing intent—arrives and causes widespread devastation. Xu expresses regret, acknowledging that sending Wang Lin there may have been a mistake, given the chaos it unleashed.
