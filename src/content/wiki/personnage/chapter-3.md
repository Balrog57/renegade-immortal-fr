## Chapter 3: Test

The selection for new disciples begins under the supervision of Immortal Hui Bing , a middle-aged cultivator from the Heng Yue Sect . One by one, the village youths are tested by having his hand placed on their heads to judge their spiritual aptitude.

Ten youths fail in a row, and none are deemed qualified—until Wang Zhuo , previously arrogant but now nervous, steps forward. Hui Bing’s expression changes upon sensing Wang Zhuo’s potential and confirms that he is the one mentioned by his martial teacher. Wang Zhuo is accepted and told to stand to the right, basking in admiration from the others.

Next comes Wang Hao , who tries to win favor with flattery and quick thinking. Although initially judged “lacking in spirit,” he offers the immortal a sealed jade box containing a rare 300-year-old spiritual mushroom . Impressed by the gift, Hui Bing takes him as an assistant alchemist , granting him the right to cultivate.

The rest of the youths, overwhelmed with disappointment, begin to cry. Annoyed, Hui Bing orders them removed by the sect disciples, who vanish with the failed candidates in flashes of light.

When Wang Lin ’s turn arrives, he is filled with tension and the memory of his parents’ hopeful faces. He silently vows to succeed. But when the immortal tests him, he hears the dreaded words — “Not qualified.” Devastated, he joins the others who failed, his mind blank and heart heavy.

Out of all those tested, only three youths —Wang Zhuo, the young girl, and Wang Hao—stand on the right. Hui Bing tells the remaining candidates that while talent is important, perseverance matters even more. He announces a second test , in which the remaining youths must climb a long stone staircase within three days to prove their determination. Those who succeed will still have a chance to become disciples.

As Hui Bing departs with the three chosen ones, Wang Lin , though rejected, refuses to give up. Filled with resolve, he steps onto the staircase to begin the test of perseverance , determined to forge his own path toward cultivation.
